## Creating Your First Event

### Step 1: Event Basics

Start by navigating to **Events** > **Create New Event** from your dashboard. You'll be guided through a streamlined setup process:

1. **Event Details** - Enter your event name, date, time, and location
2. **Event Type** - Select from conference, trade show, concert, corporate gathering, or custom
3. **Expected Attendance** - Estimate attendee count for resource planning
4. **Duration** - Specify single-day or multi-day event schedule

<Note>
  Your expected attendance helps our system calculate baseline sustainability metrics and recommend appropriate carbon offset targets.
</Note>

### Step 2: Sustainability Configuration

EcoEvents makes it easy to embed sustainability into your event from the start:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Carbon Goals">
    Set your carbon reduction targets:
    - **Carbon Neutral** - Offset 100% of event emissions
    - **Carbon Negative** - Offset 110-150% to create positive impact
    - **Reduction Focus** - Minimize emissions without full offsetting
    - **Custom Target** - Define specific CO2e reduction goals

    The platform automatically calculates estimated emissions based on venue size, attendance, and event type.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Waste Management">
    Configure your waste reduction strategy:
    - Enable **zero-waste mode** for comprehensive waste elimination
    - Set recycling and composting targets (e.g., 90% diversion from landfill)
    - Select sustainable materials for signage, badges, and promotional items
    - Choose digital-first options to minimize printed materials
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Energy Sources">
    Optimize energy usage:
    - Prioritize venues with renewable energy certification
    - Set limits on energy-intensive equipment
    - Enable smart lighting and HVAC scheduling
    - Track real-time energy consumption during the event
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Transportation">
    Reduce travel-related emissions:
    - Promote public transportation with integrated route planning
    - Offer carbon offset options for attendee travel
    - Coordinate carpooling and shuttle services
    - Provide bicycle parking and e-vehicle charging stations
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Step 3: Venue and Logistics

Select and configure your event venue with sustainability in mind:

<Accordion title="Venue Selection">
  Browse our network of **Green-Certified Venues** featuring:
  - LEED, BREEAM, or equivalent green building certifications
  - Renewable energy infrastructure
  - Water conservation systems
  - Waste management facilities
  - Accessibility features

  Filter venues by location, capacity, certification level, and sustainability score.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Layout Planning">
  Use our interactive floor plan designer to:
  - Optimize space utilization to reduce venue energy requirements
  - Position recycling and composting stations strategically
  - Plan for natural lighting to minimize electrical usage
  - Design efficient traffic flow to reduce congestion
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Catering Configuration">
  Select sustainable catering options:
  - **Plant-Based Menus** - Reduce carbon footprint by up to 70%
  - **Local Sourcing** - Partner with suppliers within 100 miles
  - **Seasonal Ingredients** - Optimize for lowest environmental impact
  - **Zero-Waste Packaging** - Eliminate single-use plastics
  - **Portion Control** - Minimize food waste with accurate planning

  Our AI-powered system estimates meal quantities based on historical data and RSVP patterns.
</Accordion>

- **Timeline Creation** - Set milestones with sustainability checkpoints

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="Start with Clear Objectives">
  Define your sustainability priorities before configuring event settings:
  - What's your primary environmental concern? (carbon, waste, water, etc.)
  - What level of certification or recognition are you targeting?
  - How will you communicate sustainability efforts to attendees?
  - What's your budget for eco-friendly upgrades vs. traditional options?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Engage Attendees Early">
  Build excitement and participation:
  - Share your sustainability goals in event marketing materials
  - Educate attendees on how they can contribute (e.g., transportation choices)
  - Gamify sustainability with challenges and rewards
  - Provide clear signage and instructions on-site
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Measure Everything">
  You can't improve what you don't measure:
  - Enable all tracking features from day one
  - Set up automated data collection for energy, waste, and water
  - Compare your metrics against industry benchmarks
  - Use historical data to improve future events
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Partner with Certified Vendors">
  Work with suppliers who share your values:
  - Verify sustainability certifications and credentials
  - Request transparency on sourcing and practices
  - Build long-term relationships for consistency
  - Provide feedback to help vendors improve
</Accordion>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="How long does it take to set up a new event?">
  Basic event setup takes 5-10 minutes. Comprehensive sustainability configuration adds another 15-20 minutes. For complex multi-day events with extensive vendor coordination, plan for 1-2 hours of initial setup.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I modify sustainability goals after the event is created?">
  Yes, but with limitations. You can adjust goals up to 30 days before the event. After that, major changes may impact vendor commitments and should be discussed with your account manager.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if my venue doesn't have green certification?">
  You can still host sustainable events at non-certified venues. EcoEvents provides tools to measure and optimize sustainability factors you can control (waste, catering, materials, transportation) even if the venue itself isn't certified.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I handle hybrid or virtual components?">
  Hybrid events significantly reduce carbon footprint by limiting travel. Enable our virtual event features to track online participation, estimate emissions saved, and provide sustainable options for remote attendees (e.g., offsetting streaming energy use).
</Accordion>
